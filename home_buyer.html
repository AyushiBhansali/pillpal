A peek into Pills

Dosage Adherence

Always follow the prescribed dosage as directed by your healthcare professional. Avoid self-medicating or altering the dosage without consulting a doctor.

Allergies and Reactions

Be aware of any allergies you may have to medications or their components. Inform your healthcare provider about any adverse reactions or allergies to prevent potential complications.

Potential Drug Interactions

Be cautious about drug interactions. Certain medications can interact unfavourably with others, including over-the-counter drugs, herbal supplements, or food. Consult a healthcare professional before combining medications.

Storage Guidelines

Store medications properly, adhering to recommended conditions (temperature, humidity) to maintain their effectiveness. Keep them out of reach of children and pets.

Pregnancy and Lactation

Inform your healthcare provider if you are pregnant, planning to conceive, or breastfeeding. Some medications can have adverse effects on the foetus or pass into breast milk.

Check Expiry Dates

Ensure that you're using medications within their expiry dates. Expired medications might not be effective and can be potentiallyÂ harmful.

Alcohol and Medications

Check for any interactions between alcohol and your medication. Some drugs can have adverse effects when combined with alcohol.

Disposal

Dispose of unused or expired medications properly. Follow local guidelines or consult a pharmacist on how to dispose of them safely to prevent accidental ingestion.
